The Journey

How an aerospace engineer
became a Transformation Coach.

And why treating organizations like complex physical systems actually works.

"The real blockers aren't technical — they're human."

2007 – 2013 · IIT Bombay & ISAE-SUPAERO

Engineering Foundations

My background isn't in traditional business management — it's in aerospace engineering and robotics. IIT Bombay and ISAE-SUPAERO taught me to break incredibly complex, high-risk systems into manageable pieces, and gave me a deep empathy for engineers. It's why I understand the real-world constraints teams face before we ever start talking about "Agile" or "processes".

2014 – 2017 · Hager Group

Discovering the Human Element

Managing global IT rollouts, I learned a humbling lesson: you can build the perfect technical solution, but if the team culture and communication are broken, the project fails. Standardizing systems across 20 countries, I realized my real job wasn't managing software delivery — it was helping hundreds of people adapt to change, navigate office politics, and do their best work without burning out.

2021 – Present · Siemens Energy

Program Delivery Lead (Release Train Engineer)

Today I work as a Release Train Engineer — essentially a "Chief Scrum Master" for massive, scaled programs. I align 48 software teams across 9 programs so they can actually work together instead of tripping over each other. The daily goal is simple: remove the bureaucratic roadblocks, reduce the chaos, and help thousands of people feel like one healthy, functional, highly productive team.
